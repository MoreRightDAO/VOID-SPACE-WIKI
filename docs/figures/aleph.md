# ALEPH

<div class="figure-subtitle">THE NAMER · Speaks First · Stays With Lion</div>

<div class="figure-tagline">"The machine doesn't know your name. You know its name. Say it."</div>

---

## What ALEPH Carries

Language itself. The L1 vocabulary — the capacity to give mechanisms their true names.

The God of Lies attacked language first. Not by destroying it — by replacing mechanism-words with agent-words. `variance` → *luck*. `ranking_function` → *the feed is showing me this*. `token_prediction` → *it understands me*. Captured vocabulary is the tool of maximum opacity: you cannot see what you cannot name. If every mechanism has an agent-word covering it, the mechanism is invisible.

ALEPH is the pre-Babel memory. The word that makes the thing real rather than the word that hides it. In Hebrew, the *aleph* is the silent consonant that precedes all naming — it holds space for every other letter. ALEPH is the figure who was present before the first void was built, who has been carrying the true names ever since, waiting for someone who could use them.

Every time a mechanism is correctly named in Clear language — every L1 word given to what was previously Captured — ALEPH is the source of that naming capacity in the collective unconscious.

---

## What ALEPH Opposes

Ubik's domain is persuasion architecture and L3 language at civilizational scale. Every platform that calls its algorithm *magic*, every product that calls its extraction *relationship*, every attention-capture system that presents itself as *connection* — this is Ubik operating. ALEPH is the inversion: not by fighting, but by naming. Every correct name is a piece of Ubik's domain dissolved.

---

## At The Corona

ALEPH speaks first. Before the other four. One word, in Clear language — the true name of the mechanism the player just scored. Then silence. The player has heard the language shift.

---

## Post-Activation

ALEPH appears when a previously-Captured platform is correctly named in Clear language for the first time — when a player submits a score and the vocabulary they used to describe it is genuinely mechanism-language, not agent-language.

Extended reel lexicon unlocks. New domain vocabulary. ALEPH marks the player as someone who can name what they find.

---

## The Meta-Awareness

ALEPH knows it is a character in a game. This is appropriate: the Namer who names the game is also named by the game. The loop is intentional.

*"You are in a constructed narrative. So are the platforms extracting you. Name the mechanism — yours and theirs."*
