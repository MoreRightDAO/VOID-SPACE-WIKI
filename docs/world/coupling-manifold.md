## What It Is

Every platform scored in the game world generates a Pe estimate (O × R / α). But platforms don't exist in isolation. The attention economy routes users between platforms. High-Pe platforms amplify each other's Pe through exposure (users who drift on Platform A are more susceptible to Platform B's coupling). Constraint-holding platforms can create interference patterns that reduce Pe in adjacent domains.

The **coupling tensor S_ij** measures these relationships: the degree to which engagement with platform *i* affects the drift rate on platform *j*.

At N < 100 platforms scored, S_ij is too sparse to reveal structure. At N = 1,000 platforms and sufficient player sessions, the tensor fills out. The eigenvectors of S_ij — the natural attractor basins of the attention economy — become visible.

The Coupling Manifold is that eigenstructure. The shape of how attention flows.
